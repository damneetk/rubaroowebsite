Circle of stories







‘Circle of Stories’ is a specially designed programme for adolescents to provide them with an opportunity to have fun, participate in out-bound adventure by experiencing a rural community and living in that space to learn about a completely different lifestyle and reality. This exposure gives young people an opportunity to question and analyse many things around them; What are my choices? What are my values? What is development? What is my role as a citizen of any community that I may be a part of [could be friends and family, even]? What are my opinions influenced by? What do I know about friendships and relationships? What are the issues I’m passionate about? As part of this immersive process, they learn about themselves, their leadership styles, and what it means to work with others.


Want to run this programme for your students :

The outbound camp camp can be customised according to your school requirement. We can take a minimum of 30 students to max 80 students at one time. It is usually 3 to max 5 days camp. 2 facilitators are assigned for 25-30 group size. We do all the safety assessment before organizing the camp.


Rural In-turn-ship







The three phases to this in-turn-ship programme are :

1. The Orientation Camp (OC): All the volunteers who go for the in-turn-ship will participate in a 5-6 day orientation camp in partnership with a grassroots organization in Telangana or Andhra Pradesh, to prepare them for the journey ahead. The OC community is a different setting, and the team of Rubaroo holds varied sessions to explore issues of stereotypes, identities, sustainable development, and the need to be open to learning. During this phase the volunteers have an opportunity to come face to face with many ground realities, that they may not have faced before.

2. In-turn-ship placement: As part of the programme, we place volunteers for a period of 4-6 weeks with grass-root level organizations across India.The dates of the in-turn-ship are usually during Semester/Summer/Winter holidays. This exposure component is a reflection of our belief in experiential learning as one of the best ways of learning, which allows individuals to form their own opinions, differentiate facts from fiction, and at the same time, provides the host organization with a helping hand on different projects through specific skills of the volunteer(s). The organizations may work on a diverse range of issues like substance abuse, HIV/AIDS, rights of the underprivileged children, communalism, environmental issues, and disability, among many more. a. As part of the placement, they have an assigned Rubaroo mentor who challenges them and also supports them (telephonically) through their in-turn-ship experience to facilitate learning. b. They also have a defined volunteer led project with the placement organisation that they have to complete as part of this. The students experiences and projects are evaluated as part of their academic curriculum.

3. Debriefing: Post their exposures the volunteers attend a feedback camp to share their experiences and the immense learning that they may have gained, to put their learnings into action for the future.


Our past experience so far ..

We have been running the Rural In-Turn-ship programme with Aga Khan Academy, Hyderabad since 2015. 180 students been taken on Orientation camp and 93 students placed for full internship with approx 15 placement organization till now.

Want to run this programme for your students

Best fit for students in grade 11th or 12th. The in-turn-ship experience help in having great Service learning and to support students in applying for Abroad education. We would love to discuss how this programme can be designed for your Institution.


Ladder to leadership







The curriculum is meant to impart a set of ‘Social, Emotional, Thinking’ (SET) skills to equip the students in this phase of adolescence. This is a 4-year programme where adolescents explore the thematics of Self-Awareness, Leadership, Community Exposure,and Action Project Implementation. This programme is built into academic curricula of grades 7, 8, 9, and 10. These interventions focus on engaging with classroom curricula and connecting them with realities around them. This become a great fun and break from regular syllabus during the school hours.

Suitable for: Grades 7 & 10

Time Duration: 52 hours (in a year) + 2-3 days of outbound/exposure visit each year


Rubaroo with Social Issues







This programme is designed along with schools to work on specific themes of Gender, Leadership, Civics and Politics, Social Identities, Ecology and the Environment, Culture, Economics, Development, Media, among many others. All the issues are designed with the youth at the core and the understanding of influencing social issues on them.

Suitable for: Grades: 9 & 10

Time Duration: 1 day/month


Looking Within: Leaders for Change







A Year-long programme, where adolescents engage with various components of leadership. These are intensive programmes that are built into the academic calendar with schools. These long-term interventions enable young people to be participants in a journey for sustainable growth. Through this journey, a young person is better equipped to handle real-time challenges, social pressures, and make informed choices.

Suitable for: Grades: 8 & 9

Time Duration: 52 hours (in a year)
