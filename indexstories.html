Rural In-turn-ship
“The five days Orientation Camp were an eye opener and a massive learning experience. It gave me a first hand overview and understanding of the life and hardships of the people living in the rural areas. The kinds and extent of discriminations that we witnessed over there was overwhelming and shocking. The various activities at camp were very thought provoking and helped me broaden my horizon. Continue...

It made me think from different kinds of perspectives and instigated me to think in ways I would never have thought before. It helped me come out of my comfort zone and push myself to work towards new boundaries. The camp gave me a great deal of understanding of the ways of the world, how interdependent and interlinked each aspect of the society is and unjust treatment certain sects of the society have to undergo. The shramdaan was a totally new experience. It filled me with a sense of satisfaction and fulfilment as I was finally able to give back a little to the place that had provided me shelter for the five days. I learnt a lot from the people there. They had bare minimum but still made sure we never felt uncomfortable and had all the possible comforts. The love and warmth with which they welcomed us and did not let language or any other barriers hinder their friendship and love towards us was heart touching and made me feel very special. It felt like we belonged with them. This camp also made me learn to be sensitive to others around and not be judgemental. It made me value and appreciate all the others who were at the camp along with me, for what they were. The camp made me understand that life is a lot more than money. Interacting with the Spanish students gave me another view on the same aspects that we had been exploring at camp. It also gave me an exposure to the kind of life students in Spain lead. It made me further realise the futility of the so called “language barrier”.If there is the desire there will definitely be ways to communicate, interact and form a bond with the other. No sort of barrier can restrict you.” ~ Anandini Gupta



“I gained knowledge, learned to respect each other and learned to take self-decision. Fun is a serious business. I learned that we should take our own decision and not judge anyone.”~ Md. Abrar Ahmed



Interfaith:Agaat-E-Baatcheet
It has been a beautiful journey and an amazing box of memories in my head. When I first came for the orientation day i did not have many expectations and I wanted to experience it and not think beforehand what all would happen. This journey taught me many good things, I have always been the kind of a person who would not interact with people or even bother making an eye contact.Continue...

But rubaroo me interact with strangers and it was not as bad as I thought it would be. I made many new friends who are helpful and kind. Who are understanding and respect my point of views. We had discussions about interfaith, different people and their point of views. It felt really good knowing that i am not the only one who is willing to do something about the unity of religions etc and there are many people who would bring their words into actions. Reach out to people for a better change. It was very kind and humble of Mr.Andrews to be a part of our beautiful journey, interact with us and discuss things just like he is one among us youth champions. The organizers have been amazingly coordinative and supportive. They have always understood our needs and supported us in every way possible. Now when i turn back and go through the amazing flashbacks of rubaroo journey, it gives me a satisfaction that i have atleast tried to bring a change to our indian society.-Safoora
